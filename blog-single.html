How to Plan a Youth Football Trip to Georgia

A practical checklist for coaches: opponents, schedule, accommodation, transport, and cultural experience — all packed into a smooth 4–7 day program.

Sport Solution Team Youth football tours & matches in Georgia

1) Define your goals

First decide what your team needs most: competitive matches, tournament experience, training camp, or team bonding. The goal determines the schedule and opponent level.

2) Pick the best dates

Most clubs choose 4–7 days. It’s enough time for 2–4 matches plus 1–3 training sessions and recovery. We recommend arriving one day before the first match.

Tip: Choose a month based on weather and school calendar. Spring and early autumn are especially comfortable.

3) Organize matches & referees

  • Opponents by age group and level
  • Referees and field booking
  • Safe match rules and scheduling

4) Logistics that matter

Transport and meals are the easiest places to lose time and energy. Make sure your plan includes airport pickup, team bus, athlete-friendly meals, and enough rest between matches.

5) Add a cultural day

A trip becomes unforgettable when you mix football with a cultural experience. Tbilisi city tour, mountains (Kazbegi) or seaside (Batumi) are popular options.
